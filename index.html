Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about comparing crypto swap rates.

A crypto swap is a direct exchange of one cryptocurrency for another without going through traditional buy/sell order books. You send one coin (e.g., BTC) to the swap service, and they send you the equivalent value in another coin (e.g., ETH). The entire process typically takes 5-30 minutes and requires no account creation on most no-KYC services.
Each exchange sources liquidity from different providers and market makers, applies different fee structures, and may have varying spreads. Network conditions, trading volume, and the exchange's own reserves all affect the final rate you see. This is exactly why comparing rates before swapping is so valuable.
All exchanges listed on SwapRadar are established services with years of operation and millions of completed swaps. However, as with all crypto services, do your own research. Since these are non-custodial swap services, your funds are only at risk during the brief swap window. Always double-check receiving addresses and start with smaller amounts if trying a new service.
KYC stands for "Know Your Customer" - a process where services require you to verify your identity with government-issued ID. No-KYC exchanges allow you to swap crypto without providing personal identification. This means faster swaps, better privacy, and no waiting for document verification. Note that some services may still apply KYC for unusually large transactions as required by regulations.
Rates are fetched in real time from each exchange's API. However, the final rate you receive may vary slightly due to market movement between when you see the rate and when your transaction is processed. For fixed-rate swaps, the rate is locked in at the time of the swap. For floating-rate swaps, the final amount may differ by a small margin.
SwapRadar is completely free to use. We earn a small referral commission from exchanges when you complete a swap through our links. This does not affect the rate you receive — the rates shown are the same rates you'd see going directly to the exchange. Our incentive is aligned with yours: we want to send you to the exchange with the best rate.
